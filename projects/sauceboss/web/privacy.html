SauceBoss — Privacy Policy

What we collect, where it lives, and how to delete it.
← Back to SauceBoss

Last updated: 2026-05-07

The short version

SauceBoss is a recipe app. We collect the minimum needed to let you sign in, save favorites, and contribute recipes. We don't sell your data, run ads, or share what you do in the app with anyone outside the small set of infrastructure providers that store and serve it. Delete your account from Settings → Delete account at any time and we wipe your profile and favorites immediately.

What we collect

If you sign in

  • Email address — required for account creation. Used to identify your account and (if you sign in with Google) supplied by Google to our authentication provider.
  • Display name — defaults to the part of your email before the @. Editable from Settings. Never displayed to other users; only shown to you.
  • Favorite recipes — the list of sauce IDs you've hearted.
  • Recipes you author — sauces you create through the in-app builder. The recipe content is public to other users; your association as the author is stored privately and used only to show edit/delete controls on the recipes you own.
  • Authentication tokens — issued by Supabase Auth so you stay signed in. Stored on your device in Apple Keychain (iOS) or the Android Keystore.

If you sign in with Google

We receive your email address and your account's basic profile info (name, avatar URL) from Google. We don't request any additional Google scopes — no Drive, no Calendar, no contacts.

If you don't sign in

We collect no personal data. Browsing recipes, filtering by ingredients, viewing the pie charts — all of that works anonymously.

Anonymous app-open ping

Each time the app opens, it sends a single anonymous request to our backend recording that an "app_open" event happened. No user identifier, no device fingerprint, no IP address logging beyond the standard server access logs that our hosting provider rotates and discards.

Where it lives

SauceBoss data is stored across three providers, all in the United States and EU:

  • Supabase — authentication, account profiles, favorites, sauces. Encrypted in transit (TLS) and at rest. Backed up daily by Supabase.
  • Railway — the API server that brokers requests to Supabase. No user data is persisted on Railway; requests pass through.
  • Vercel — hosts the web version of SauceBoss and this privacy policy. No user data is sent to Vercel.

We don't share your data with anyone besides these three providers, and we don't use third-party analytics, tracking pixels, or advertising SDKs.

What we don't collect

  • No location data.
  • No contacts, calendar, photos, or other device data.
  • No device identifiers (no IDFA, no Android Advertising ID).
  • No analytics beyond the anonymous app-open ping above.
  • No payment information — SauceBoss is free with no in-app purchases.

Your rights

Edit

Change your display name from Settings → Display name.

Delete

From Settings → Delete account. This immediately removes your profile row and every favorite associated with it. Recipes you authored stay in the catalog (so they don't disappear from other users mid-meal) but lose their owner attribution — they become unowned and can't be edited by anyone other than an admin.

If you'd prefer your authored recipes deleted as well, email us before deleting the account and we'll handle it manually.

Export

Email us at the address below and we'll send you a JSON dump of everything associated with your account, normally within 30 days.

Children

SauceBoss is not directed at children under 13. We don't knowingly collect personal data from anyone under 13. If you believe a child has signed up, email us and we'll remove the account.

Cookies and storage

The native app stores authentication tokens in your device's secure storage (Keychain / Keystore) and caches reference data (cuisine categories, ingredient substitutions) in memory for the session. The web version does the same via browser localStorage rather than cookies. We don't set any third-party tracking cookies.

Encryption and export

SauceBoss uses standard, generally available cryptography (HTTPS / TLS for transport, JWT for sessions, OS-provided secure storage for tokens). It does not contain or use any non-mass-market encryption. The app is classified under ECCN 5D992.c and qualifies for License Exception ENC under §740.17(b)(1) of the US Export Administration Regulations.

SauceBoss is not distributed to countries embargoed by the United States.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes materially, we'll bump the "Last updated" date at the top and surface a notice the next time you open the app. Minor wording fixes won't get a notice.
